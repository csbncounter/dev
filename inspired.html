What is N-Counter?

Once in a Battalion Career

N-Counter is a multi-regional CSB Battalion event, which aims to give Battalion guys a rare encounter with other Battalions from across the country. Young men have been involved in their own group from day one; some have also participated with other Battalions in their region through annual Camp-O-Ramas. This event is different, and comes once in a Brigadier’s career.

The purpose of CSB N-Counter is to glorify God through fellowship, discipleship, use of the CSB Battalion Program on a multi-regional scale over a single weekend, April 29 - May 1, 2016, at High Point Camp near Birdsboro, PA.

Men who have mentored their Brigadiers toward following Him all year bring them to an event where Jesus is glorified in spirit and in truth. They share in common the purpose of CSB, building godly men of today and tomorrow, by serving and leading others to become disciples across the generations.

Battalions from regions far and wide bring an activity, a skill, a challenge, or a competition to share with the others. Every young man heads into a non-stop adventure of one new experience after the next together with old friends and new. Think “everything that is awesome about Battalion” in one place for one weekend.

In the evening, all gather under one roof for a passionate worship ceremony followed by an awe-inspiring outdoor torchlight and bonfire event that leaves no one without personally encountering God. This year the theme is “Press On” based on Philippians 3:12-16.

Without any other event to compare to, the only way to get a taste of N-Counter is to see for yourself what is proving to be even bigger in 2016. Don’t miss this “once in a Battalion career” event.

Facts from 2011

  • A total of 472 young men attended the N-Counter 2011 event hosted at High Point Camp.

  • The 472 attendees represented 29 battalion units located throughout 7 States.

  • Over the course of a weekend, 30 or more young men prayed to step out in faith and accept Christ to be their personal Lord and Savior.

  • An induction ceremony was held for 4 new Heralds of Christ recipients and 3 new Heralds of Christ for Men recipients.
